A Chicago man is accused of changing UPS headquarters postal address to his one-bedroom apartment and stealing the company’s mail – including $58,000 in checks. For nearly three months, Dushaun Henderson-Spruce allegedly redirected mail from the company in Atlanta to his own home about 700 miles away, the Chicago Tribune reported. Henderson-Spruce, 24, pulled off the scam by submitting a written U.S. Postal Service change of address form Oct. 26, according to officials. The alleged con man then began to receive such volumes of mail that the carrier reportedly would leave the delivery in tubs at his building.
